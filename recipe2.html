History

While the origin of the French fry isn’t entirely clear, historians often credit it to France or Belgium.
After being introduced to Europe by the Spanish, the potato gradually spread throughout the continent.
It’s believed that Belgians began the process of frying strips of potatoes between the late 17th and early
18th centuries. When the river would freeze over each winter, and fishing was not to be had, the potato
became a stand-in for the fish, and the earliest iteration of French fries was born.

Description

Skip the drive-thru and make irresistible seasoned fries at home. This recipe creates fries that are crispy on the outside and fluffy on the inside.

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ pounds russet potatoes, peeled
  • 1 cup vegetable oil for frying
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic salt
  • 1 teaspoon onion salt
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup water, or as needed

Tips/Notes

  • Cut the potatoes evenly to ensure uniform cooking.
  • Make sure to pat the fries dry to avoid oil splatter.
  • Don’t overcrowd the frying pan, fry in small batches.

Instructions

  1. Slice potatoes into French fries and place them into a large
    bowl of cold water to prevent them from turning brown.
  2. Heat oil in a heavy saucepan or large skillet to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  3. While the oil is heating, sift flour, garlic salt, onion salt, paprika,
    and salt into a large bowl. Gradually stir in enough water to make
    a slightly thick batter that can be drizzled from a spoon. Mix well
  4. Drain fries and pat dry; dip into the batter one at a time.
  5. Lower fries carefully into the hot oil in batches to prevent them from clumping together. Fry until golden brown and crispy on all sides, about 7 to 10 minutes.
  6. Drain fries on a paper towel-lined plate.
  7. Serve hot and enjoy!
